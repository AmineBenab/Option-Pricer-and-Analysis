# Options Pricer

A Python-based options pricing framework implementing three valuation methods : Black-Scholes, Binomial CRR, and Monte Carlo - with full Greeks computation, convergence analysis, put-call parity verification, early exercise premium analysis, and 3D implied volatility surface generation via numerical inversion.

---

## Features

- **Black-Scholes** - analytical closed-form pricing for European calls and puts, with all 5 Greeks (Delta, Gamma, Vega, Theta, Rho) computed analytically
- **Binomial CRR** - recombining tree with backward induction, supporting both European and American exercise, convergent to Black-Scholes as n → ∞
- **Monte Carlo** - direct GBM terminal price sampling across 10,000+ simulations, no path discretization required
- **Convergence analysis** - empirical verification of O(1/n) convergence for CRR and O(1/√n) for Monte Carlo against Black-Scholes benchmark
- **Greeks profiles** - full visualization of Delta, Gamma, Vega, Theta, Rho as a function of spot price for calls and puts side-by-side
- **Put-Call Parity** - empirical verification that C − P = S·e^(−qT) − K·e^(−rT) holds exactly under Black-Scholes
- **Early Exercise Premium** - American vs European price difference across spot range and volatility range, for both calls (dividend-driven) and puts (rate-driven)
- **Implied Volatility Surface** - 3D surface across 120+ strike/maturity combinations with realistic skew, generated by numerical inversion of Black-Scholes via `scipy.optimize.brentq`

---

## Key Concepts

| Concept | Implementation |
|---|---|
| Geometric Brownian Motion | GBM exact solution for MC terminal price |
| Risk-neutral pricing | Discounted expectation under Q-measure |
| CRR calibration | u = e^(σ√Δt), d = 1/u, p = (e^(r−q)Δt − d)/(u−d) |
| Backward induction | American: max(continuation, intrinsic value) |
| Put-Call Parity | C − P = S·e^(−qT) − K·e^(−rT) |
| Volatility skew | σ(K) = σ_ATM + skew × (S−K)/S |
| Numerical inversion | brentq root-finding on bs_price(σ) − market_price = 0 |

## Results

### Pricing Comparison (S=100, K=100, σ=20%, T=1y, r=3%, q=2%)

| | Black-Scholes | CRR (n=100) | Monte Carlo (N=10,000) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Call EU | 8.27 | 8.25 | ~8.27 ± 0.08 |
| Put EU | 7.29 | 7.27 | ~7.29 ± 0.08 |
| Call US | 8.27 | 8.25 | — |
| Put US | 7.29 | 7.39 | — |

The put American premium of ~0.10€ reflects the value of early exercise when the option is deep ITM.

### Convergence Rates

| Method | Theoretical | Empirical (log-log slope) |
|---|---|---|
| Binomial CRR | O(1/n) | ≈ −1.0 |
| Monte Carlo | O(1/√n) | ≈ −0.5 |

### Implied Volatility Surface
A skew surface is generated across 120+ strike/maturity combinations by imposing σ(K) = σ_ATM + skew × (S−K)/S on simulated market prices, then recovering each implied vol via `brentq`. Low-liquidity options (price < 0.5% of spot) are filtered to ensure numerical stability of the inversion.
