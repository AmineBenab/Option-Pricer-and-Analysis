# Options Pricer

A Python-based options pricing framework implementing three valuation methods — Black-Scholes, Binomial CRR, and Monte Carlo — along with Greeks computation, convergence analysis, and implied volatility surface generation.

---

## Features

- **Black-Scholes** analytical pricing with closed-form Greeks (Delta, Gamma, Vega, Theta, Rho)
- **Binomial CRR** tree with support for both European and American options
- **Monte Carlo** simulation via direct GBM terminal price sampling
- **Convergence analysis** — binomial and Monte Carlo vs Black-Scholes benchmark
- **Greeks profiles** — visualization of all five Greeks as a function of spot price
- **Put-Call Parity** — empirical verification against theoretical formula
- **Early Exercise Premium** — American vs European price difference across spot and volatility
- **Implied Volatility Surface** — 3D surface with skew via numerical inversion of Black-Scholes

## Results

### Pricing Convergence
The binomial CRR converges to Black-Scholes in **O(1/n)** while Monte Carlo converges in **O(1/√n)** — confirming the theoretical convergence rates empirically.

### Early Exercise Premium
The put American premium is maximized when the option is deep ITM and when dividends are low. The call premium only appears with significant dividend yields.

### Implied Volatility Surface
A realistic skew surface is generated by imposing a linear skew on simulated market prices, then inverting Black-Scholes numerically via `scipy.optimize.brentq`.
